Biography

Dr. Attia Youseif is a Senior Lecturer of Arabic in the Department of Middle East Languages and Cultures at Indiana University Bloomington. His work focuses on Arabic linguistics, Arabic grammar and morphology, Arabic language pedagogy, Arabic dialectology, and Arabic cultural studies.

He holds a Ph.D. in Arabic Linguistics from Alexandria University (2008), an M.A. in Arabic Grammar and Morphology (2003), and a Diploma in Teaching Arabic to Non-Native Speakers (2010). He has taught Arabic at novice, intermediate, advanced, and superior levels across university and intensive language settings.

In addition to teaching and curriculum design, Dr. Youseif serves as Director of Tutoring of the Arabic Flagship Program. His research and materials development include corpus-based Arabic learning resources, interactive digital learning environments, and computational analysis of Arabic literary and cultural texts.
